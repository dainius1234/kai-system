# Skill: Ladder and Working at Height Safety (UK)

## Overview

Working at Height Regulations 2005 (WAH Regs) govern all work where a person could fall
a distance liable to cause personal injury. Ladders are only the last resort — hierarchy
of control requires you to consider scaffolding, MEWPs, and other collective protection
first. Most falls from height in UK construction are preventable and potentially fatal.

## Key rules / facts

- WAH Regs apply to **any height** — even a step ladder at 0.5m if a fall could injure
- Hierarchy: eliminate the need to work at height → collective protection (scaffolding, edge protection) → personal protection (PPE, harness) → ladders as LAST resort
- Ladders are only suitable for **light, short-duration work** (≤30 minutes per task per BS 8620 guidance) or where assessment shows other equipment is unreasonable
- **3-point contact** must be maintained at all times when climbing
- Angle: **1 in 4 rule** (75° from horizontal) — 1 foot out for every 4 feet up
- Leaning ladders must be secured: tied at top, footed at bottom, or both
- Step ladders must be fully open and locked — never use folded
- Inspection required before each use (PUWER) — check stiles, rungs, feet, locking mechanisms
- Ladders must be footed by a second person if not tied
- Maximum load rating must not be exceeded (check label — typically 150kg for class 1 industrial)

## Common mistakes / gotchas

- Overreaching — keep your belt buckle between the stiles
- Using a ladder near an unguarded edge without additional fall protection
- Standing on the top 3 rungs of a leaning ladder or top 2 steps of a step ladder
- Not inspecting ladders that have been in a van (vibration can cause hidden damage)
- Using a ladder on soft or uneven ground without a stability device or leveller
- Not recording ladder inspections — PUWER requires records for equipment in use

## Reference values

| Item | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ladder angle | 75° (1:4) | 1 out for 4 up |
| Max duration (light work) | 30 min | Per task — not per day |
| Min overlap for extension ladders | 3 rungs | Manufacturer may require more |
| Class EN 131 | ≤150kg | Check label — heavier workers need Class 1 |
| Inspection frequency | Every use | Plus formal periodic inspection |
| Top rungs to avoid (leaning) | Top 3 | Standing on top 3 = unsafe |
| Top steps to avoid (step) | Top 2 | Manufacturer's guidance |

## Worked examples

### Example 1 — Is a ladder appropriate?

**Situation:** You need to fix a fascia board 4m up the wall. Work takes 45 minutes.
**Assessment:** 45 minutes exceeds the 30-minute guidance for ladder use. Work involves fixing (hands-off-ladder time). Recommendation: use a podium step or tower scaffold for this task. Document your risk assessment reasoning.

### Example 2 — Setting up a leaning ladder

**Situation:** 6m leaning ladder, smooth concrete floor, brick wall.
- Base distance from wall: 6m ÷ 4 = **1.5m** from wall base
- Top: tie the stile tops to a fixed anchor (wall hook screwed into masonry, or use a ladder stay)
- Bottom: rubber feet on concrete — if any slip risk, have someone foot it throughout the task
- Check: top 3 rungs not used as standing position; don't overreach

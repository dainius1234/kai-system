# Skill: MTD and VAT (UK Self-Employed)

## Overview

Making Tax Digital (MTD) and VAT registration are the two major compliance thresholds
for UK self-employed people. MTD for Income Tax requires digital record-keeping above
£50,000 annual income. VAT registration is mandatory above £85,000 rolling 12-month
turnover. Both have preparation steps you want to start before you cross the threshold.

## Key rules / facts

- **VAT threshold**: £85,000 rolling 12-month turnover — not tax year, not calendar year
- Once you cross VAT threshold you must register within 30 days or face a penalty
- **MTD for Income Tax**: mandated from April 2026 for income >£50,000; from April 2027 for >£30,000
- MTD requires compatible software that submits quarterly updates to HMRC digitally
- VAT Flat Rate Scheme (FRS) available if VAT-registered with turnover <£150,000 — often simpler
- Standard VAT rate: 20%; construction services vary (zero-rate for new builds, 5% for certain renovations)
- Domestic Reverse Charge applies for CIS supplies between VAT-registered businesses — you don't charge/collect VAT, the customer accounts for it

## Common mistakes / gotchas

- Measuring VAT threshold against tax year instead of rolling 12 months — the 12-month test means you can breach it mid-year
- Voluntary VAT registration has advantages (reclaim VAT on purchases, looks established) — consider it before being forced
- Not notifying HMRC within 30 days of breaching VAT threshold = automatic penalty (minimum £250)
- MTD-compatible software ≠ your existing spreadsheet — must submit via API-enabled software
- FRS percentage for construction (general building/installation): 9.5% — check your specific trade

## Worked examples

### Example 1 — VAT threshold check

**Situation:** You earned £22k Jan–Dec last year and £64k Jan–Nov this year. What's your VAT position?
**Calculation:** Rolling 12 months = last Dec (£2k typical last month of £22k) + Jan–Nov this year (£64k) = ~£66k. Below threshold — but monitor December closely.

### Example 2 — Flat Rate Scheme saving

**Situation:** You're VAT-registered, general building contractor, turnover £120k.
- Standard VAT you'd collect from customers: £120k × 20% = £24k
- FRS rate for general construction: 9.5% of VAT-inclusive turnover (£144k × 9.5% = £13,680)
- You keep £24,000 − £13,680 = **£10,320** — but you cannot reclaim input VAT on purchases.

# Skill: CIS Deductions (UK Construction Industry Scheme)

## Overview

The Construction Industry Scheme (CIS) requires UK contractors to deduct money from
subcontractor payments and pass it to HMRC. As a subcontractor, these deductions count
as advance payments toward your Income Tax and NI bill. Track every deduction — you
reclaim the credit through Self Assessment.

## Key rules / facts

- **Registered subcontractor**: contractor deducts **20%** from your labour payment
- **Unregistered subcontractor**: contractor deducts **30%** — register to get the lower rate
- **Gross payment status**: 0% deduction; HMRC grants this once your compliance record is strong
- CIS applies to **labour only** — materials, plant hire, and VAT are excluded from the deduction base
- Contractors must verify you with HMRC before making the first payment
- Monthly returns due to HMRC by the 19th of each month (contractors)
- Your CIS deduction statement (CIS voucher) must come from every contractor, every month

## Common mistakes / gotchas

- Including materials in the CIS deduction base (wrong — materials are exempt)
- Forgetting to register for CIS — costs you 10p in every £1 vs. registered rate
- Not keeping CIS vouchers — you need these as proof when claiming the credit on Self Assessment
- Mixing up CIS deduction (what the contractor takes) with VAT — separate calculations entirely
- Domestic Reverse Charge (DRC) for VAT: if VAT-registered, you don't charge VAT to CIS contractors who are also VAT-registered — they account for it themselves

## Worked examples

### Example 1 — Simple labour-only payment

**Situation:** Contractor pays you £2,000 gross for brickwork (no materials). You are registered (20%).
**Calculation:**
- Labour: £2,000
- CIS deduction: £2,000 × 20% = £400
- You receive: £1,600
- The £400 goes to HMRC in your name — it's a credit on your tax bill.

### Example 2 — Mixed labour and materials

**Situation:** Contractor pays £3,000 gross for plastering. £1,200 is materials, £1,800 is labour. Registered.
**Calculation:**
- Deduction base: £1,800 (labour only — materials exempt)
- CIS deduction: £1,800 × 20% = £360
- Net payment: £3,000 − £360 = £2,640
- Materials portion passes through with no deduction.
